Cancun

Mexico · Caribbean Coast

Overview

Cancun is the quintessential spring break and graduation trip destination for a reason: it delivers more bang for your buck than almost anywhere else on earth. The Hotel Zone (Zona Hotelera) is a narrow barrier island lined with turquoise Caribbean water on one side and calm Nichupté Lagoon on the other — bikinis and all-you-can-drink wristbands from check-in to checkout.

But here's what most first-timers don't realize: Cancun is also a perfect base for some of the most extraordinary experiences in the Americas. Within 1.5 to 3 hours you can be swimming in a jade-colored cenote, standing at the base of a 1,000-year-old Mayan pyramid at Chichen Itza, or snorkeling above the MUSA underwater museum. The contrast — ancient ruins in the morning, rooftop pool at noon, legendary nightclub at midnight — is what makes it unforgettable.

June is an excellent time to visit. The crowds from spring break have cleared, prices drop slightly from peak, and the water is perfectly warm. The hurricane season technically begins June 1st but major storms in early June are rare. You'll have the beach largely to fellow travelers rather than families, and the Hotel Zone clubs are still operating at full intensity.

For a group of graduating seniors, Cancun is the most reliable choice on this list. It's close (3 hours from Chicago), no visa is required (though a valid US passport is), the US dollar stretches well, and it has literally everything: beach, adventure, culture, and one of the most legendary nightlife scenes in the hemisphere.

Top Activities

  • 🌊 Cenote Dos OjosOne of the world's great snorkeling/diving sites — two interconnected freshwater cenotes near Tulum. Crystal-clear water, surreal caverns. ~1.5 hrs from Cancun.
  • 🏛 Chichen ItzaThe most famous Mayan site in the world. El Castillo pyramid is jaw-dropping. Book an early tour to beat the heat and crowds. ~2.5 hrs from Cancun.
  • ⛵ Isla MujeresA 20-minute ferry from Puerto Juárez. Golf carts, stunning beaches, snorkeling — perfect day trip. Much less touristy than the Hotel Zone.
  • 🤿 MUSA SnorkelingThe Museo Subacuático de Arte — over 500 life-size sculptures on the ocean floor, now covered in coral. Surreal and unforgettable.
  • 🌅 Tulum RuinsCliff-top Mayan ruins overlooking the Caribbean. The setting is spectacular. Combine with a cenote visit for a full day. ~1.5 hrs south.
  • 🏄 Xel-Há / XenotesAll-inclusive aquatic parks with zip-lines, cenotes, and snorkeling. Pricey (~$80–$120) but includes food and drinks. Great for a full group day.

Food & Drink

  • 🌮 Los de PescadoBest fish tacos in the city. Cheap, fresh, and beloved by locals. Multiple locations.
  • 🍽 La HabichuelaUpscale Yucatecan cuisine in a garden setting. Famous for cocobichuela (lobster in coconut cream sauce). Treat yourself once.
  • 🏪 Mercado 28The local market downtown — cheapest food in town. Great for pozole, tamales, and fresh juice. Miles away from tourist prices.
  • 🫙 LabnáTraditional Yucatecan food in a colonial setting. Try the cochinita pibil (slow-roasted pork). Downtown location.
  • 🍹 El Fish FritangaCasual seafood spot near the Hotel Zone with great ceviche and micheladas at very fair prices.

Nightlife

  • 🎭 Coco BongoThe most famous club in Cancun — part Vegas show, part rave, part circus. Acrobats drop from the ceiling. Not subtle, completely unforgettable. Cover ~$60–$80 with open bar.
  • 🏖 Mandala Beach ClubDaytime and nighttime beach club with multiple levels, pools, and DJs. One of the best setups on the strip.
  • 🎉 Dady'OClassic Cancun mega-club that's been packing in crowds for decades. EDM and hip-hop rooms. Open until dawn.
  • 🐸 Señor Frog'sThe iconic chain — raucous, neon, party-starter energy. Best for pregaming before hitting the clubs. Right on the lagoon.
  • 🐄 La VaquitaPopular with young travelers and locals alike. Cheap drinks, no frills, great energy. Good for a budget night out.
