New Orleans

Louisiana · USA Domestic

Overview

New Orleans is the most singular city in the United States — a place where European, African, Caribbean, and Native American cultures fused into something that exists nowhere else in the world: Creole cooking, second-line parades, jazz born on Congo Square, above-ground cemeteries, shotgun houses draped in wisteria, and a relationship with pleasure that is deeply, philosophically different from anywhere else in America. For a group of graduating seniors, it is the most culturally rich option on this list, and it is by far the cheapest.

The food alone justifies the trip. New Orleans has produced a culinary tradition unlike anything else in North America — gumbo, jambalaya, étouffée, beignets, po'boys, muffulettas, bananas Foster, bread pudding with whiskey sauce. These are not tourist approximations; this is what people actually eat. Commander's Palace has been one of the finest restaurants in America for 50 years. Central Grocery invented the muffuletta in 1906 and is still making them. Café Du Monde has served café au lait and beignets at the edge of Jackson Square around the clock since 1862.

The key local knowledge for nightlife: skip Bourbon Street as your main destination (it's loud, tacky, and full of tourists), and head instead to Frenchmen Street in the Faubourg Marigny neighborhood, just outside the French Quarter. Frenchmen is where New Orleans actually lives — multiple clubs and bars with live jazz, blues, and brass bands running simultaneously, low cover charges or none at all, and a crowd of locals and in-the-know visitors. The Spotted Cat, d.b.a., and the Maison are all within a block of each other. It is one of the finest nightlife streets in America.

One genuine warning: June in New Orleans is brutal. The heat index regularly exceeds 105°F, and the humidity is absolute. Plan outdoor activities (Garden District walk, cemetery tours) for early morning. Embrace the air conditioning midday, and save your energy for evening, when the city genuinely comes alive. The off-peak summer prices are a real benefit — hotels and Airbnbs that cost $300/night during Mardi Gras can be had for $80–$120 in June.

Top Activities

  • 🎺 Frenchmen StreetThe real New Orleans music scene. Multiple clubs with live jazz, blues, and brass bands simultaneously. Start at The Spotted Cat, then wander. Best after 10pm.
  • 🍩 Café Du MondePowdered sugar beignets and café au lait at the edge of Jackson Square, open 24 hours. A pilgrimage. Eat outside at 2am and watch the street performers.
  • 🏘 Garden District WalkAntebellum mansions, gothic oaks dripping with Spanish moss, and Anne Rice's former house. Take the St. Charles Avenue streetcar and walk back through the neighborhood.
  • 🐊 Bayou Swamp TourAirboat or flatboat tours through the cypress swamps of Jean Lafitte National Park, 45 minutes south of the city. Alligators, egrets, and bayou silence.
  • 🏛 National WWII MuseumOne of the finest museums in America, period. The 4D film narrated by Tom Hanks alone is worth the visit. Budget 3–4 hours.
  • ⚜ Cemetery TourSt. Louis Cemetery No. 1 requires a guided tour (required by law). Above-ground tombs, voodoo history, and Nicolas Cage's pre-purchased pyramid vault. Genuinely fascinating.

Food & Drink

  • 🍽 Commander's PalaceThe grand dame of New Orleans cuisine. The jazz brunch on Saturday is legendary and more affordable than dinner. Garden District. Dress code required.
  • 🥖 Central GroceryThe birthplace of the muffuletta — an enormous Italian-Creole sandwich on round sesame bread with olive salad. A must. French Quarter, opens at 9am.
  • 🦐 CochonThe finest Cajun restaurant in the city, from chef Donald Link. Boudin, cracklins, cochon de lait. Warehouse District. Book ahead.
  • 🍤 Domilise's Po-BoysA New Orleans institution in Uptown. Shrimp po-boy on fresh French bread. Simple, perfect, cheap, and beloved by locals for 90 years.
  • 🥃 Sazerac BarThe Roosevelt Hotel's legendary bar, birthplace of the Sazerac cocktail. Order a Sazerac and a Pimm's Cup and feel the history of the room.

Nightlife

  • 🎷 The Spotted CatThe quintessential Frenchmen Street experience — tiny, packed, live jazz until late, free admission. Get there early enough to get close to the band.
  • 🎸 Tipitina'sThe legendary venue in Uptown where the Neville Brothers and Dr. John made history. Still books the best local acts. Cover varies ($10–$25).
  • 🎵 Preservation HallTraditional New Orleans jazz in a cramped, beautiful, historic room. Three 45-minute sets per night. $25 general admission. Absolutely essential.
  • 🍸 d.b.a.Frenchmen Street bar with excellent craft cocktails, live music, and a back patio. One of the best bars in New Orleans for a long, relaxed evening.
  • 🎉 Bourbon StreetLoud, touristy, and completely over the top — worth one night for the experience. Hand grenades, go-cups, and whatever else the night brings.

Practical Tips

HeatGenuinely dangerous in June. Hydrate constantly, wear light clothing, plan breaks.
Go CupsOpen container is legal on the street in most of the city. Embrace the culture.
Getting AroundSt. Charles streetcar is the most scenic way to get around. Uber/Lyft for late night.
NeighborhoodsFrench Quarter and Marigny safe for tourists. Avoid wandering into unknown areas late at night.
