Cabo San Lucas

Baja California Sur · Mexico

Overview

Cabo San Lucas sits at the very tip of the Baja California peninsula, where the Sonoran Desert tumbles dramatically into the Sea of Cortez. The landscape is unlike anything else on earth: cactus-studded mountains behind you, turquoise water in front, and El Arco — a 200-foot natural rock arch at Land's End — framing the whole scene from the water. It's more compact and manageable than Cancun, with a tighter concentration of beaches, restaurants, and nightlife around the marina and Medano Beach.

Cabo's great advantage over Cancun is its diversity of experience within a small area. Medano Beach is the main party beach — swim-up bars, jet skis, volleyball — but 10 minutes by water taxi gets you to Lover's Beach and Divorce Beach, quiet coves accessible only by boat, where you can snorkel and watch sea lions on the rocks at Land's End. A 45-minute drive north puts you in the desert for ATV and UTV tours through dramatic volcanic terrain and past towering saguaro cactus. The contrast between desert adventure and beach party within a single day is Cabo's secret weapon.

The nightlife is smaller-scale than Cancun but has its own iconic venues. Cabo Wabo Cantina — founded by Van Halen's Sammy Hagar — is a rock-and-roll institution that books live bands most nights. Squid Roe is the old-school party bar where margaritas are poured directly into your mouth from a squeeze bottle (you've been warned, or enticed). Mandala is the serious EDM club if you want something more contemporary.

June is actually an ideal time to visit Cabo. Spring break crowds are long gone, the prices haven't peaked for summer, and the desert is dry and hot (90s°F) but the Pacific side of the beach has a cooling breeze. Hurricane risk in June is genuinely low — the season officially begins but major storms in early June are very rare. No visa required (a valid US passport is needed), and the US dollar goes far, especially outside the resort zone.

Top Activities

  • 🏔 El Arco Boat TourA glass-bottom boat tour to Land's End and the famous arch. Watch sea lions sunning on the rocks. One of the most iconic photo ops in Mexico. ~$15–$25 per person.
  • 🤿 Lover's Beach SnorkelOnly accessible by water taxi from the marina (~$5). Snorkel around the arch, explore two beaches — calm Lover's Beach on the Sea of Cortez side, and rough Divorce Beach on the Pacific.
  • 🏍 Desert ATV TourHalf-day UTV tours through the Baja desert. Dramatic volcanic landscapes, cactus fields, mountain views. Multiple operators near downtown. ~$80–$120 pp.
  • ⛵ Sunset SailingVarious catamarans and sailing yachts offer sunset cruises from the marina, often with open bar included. One of the best ways to spend an evening.
  • 🎣 Deep-Sea FishingThe Sea of Cortez is one of the world's great sport fishing destinations. Charters go for marlin, tuna, and dorado. Half-day trips ~$100–$150 pp.
  • 🐬 Dolphin & Whale SharkJune is whale shark season in the Sea of Cortez (particularly near La Paz, 2 hours away). Tours from Cabo Pulmo offer snorkeling with the largest fish in the world.

Food & Drink

  • 🏖 The OfficeLiterally on the sand at Medano Beach — tables in the water, feet in the sand, legendary for Sunday afternoons. Classic Mexican food, strong margaritas, great vibe.
  • 🌮 Taco stands (Marina)The best and cheapest food in Cabo is from the taco stands near the marina and downtown. Baja fish tacos with cabbage slaw are the regional specialty.
  • 🍽 Mi Casa RestaurantReliable upscale Mexican in a beautiful courtyard downtown. Great for a nicer group dinner — Yucatecan specialties and fresh seafood.
  • 🍺 Baja Brewing CoCabo's best craft brewery with a great outdoor terrace. Good burgers, excellent local beer, and a crowd that mixes travelers and locals.
  • 🎉 El Squid RoePart restaurant, part carnival — three levels of loud music, flaming shots, and the kind of chaos that is either your favorite or least favorite thing in the world.

Nightlife

  • 🎸 Cabo Wabo CantinaThe most famous bar in Cabo, founded by Sammy Hagar of Van Halen. Live rock bands most nights, great sound system, outdoor stage. An institution.
  • 🎧 Mandala CaboThe contemporary nightclub of choice — EDM programming, multiple levels, proper production. Opens at 10pm, fills up around midnight.
  • 🍹 Nowhere BarLaid-back bar right on the marina. Cold drinks, good music, and no pretension. Perfect for starting the night without spending a lot.
  • 🏖 Medano Beach ClubsThe swim-up bars at Mango Deck and Monkey Business on Medano Beach run day parties that blend seamlessly into evenings.
