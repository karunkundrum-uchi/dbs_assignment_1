Punta Cana

Dominican Republic · Caribbean

Overview

Punta Cana's pitch is elegantly simple: pay one price, go somewhere beautiful, and never have to worry about another thing all week. The all-inclusive model — meals, drinks, entertainment, and most activities included in a single nightly rate — makes it the most stress-free and group-friendly destination on this list. There's no currency to figure out, no tipping calculation at every meal, no navigating which bar has the best deal. You check in, you put on a wristband, and the Caribbean does the rest.

The beaches here are genuinely exceptional. Bávaro Beach stretches for miles — powder-white sand, absurdly turquoise water, gentle waves from the Atlantic. The resort strip has developed to serve literally millions of visitors per year, which means the infrastructure is excellent (pools, spas, beach volleyball, watersports, nightly shows) even if the surroundings lack the authenticity of Cancun or Cabo. For a graduation trip where the goal is relaxation and fun rather than cultural immersion, that's a perfectly reasonable trade.

The single best excursion from Punta Cana is the Saona Island catamaran trip: a half-day sail along the coast to a pristine island off the southeastern tip of the Dominican Republic, with snorkeling stops and a visit to a natural pool in the open ocean where starfish cover the sandbar. Every resort can book it, and nearly every repeat visitor calls it the highlight of their trip. It is not to be missed.

The one significant logistics note: Punta Cana is the only destination on this list that requires a US passport. You'll also need to fill out a free Dominican tourist e-ticket online before traveling (replaced the physical tourist card). June is technically hurricane season but historically very calm — and it's low season, which means the all-inclusive prices are at their annual minimum. A week at a decent resort in June can cost significantly less than the same room in January.

Top Activities

  • ⛵ Saona Island TripThe must-do excursion: catamaran to Saona Island, snorkeling stops, open bar, BBQ lunch on the beach, and a natural starfish pool. Book through your resort or any local tour operator. ~$65–$90 pp.
  • 🏍 Dune Buggy / ATVOff-road buggy tours through sugar cane fields, cacao plantations, and Dominican villages — with a natural pool and waterfall stop. About 3 hours, hugely popular with groups. ~$75–$100 pp.
  • 🤿 Scuba / SnorkelingThe Cortecito reef is one of the most accessible in the Caribbean. Many resorts have dive shops on-site. Night dives are also offered.
  • 🪂 Scape ParkMulti-adventure park near Cap Cana with zip-lines, underground cenote, indigenous cave, and water activities. Full-day option. ~$80–$130 pp.
  • 🐴 Horseback Beach RideMultiple operators offer sunrise or sunset horseback rides along Bávaro Beach. Magical at golden hour. ~$40–$60 pp.
  • 🎣 Deep-Sea FishingThe DR's waters are excellent for mahi-mahi, wahoo, and marlin. Half-day charter from Marina Cap Cana, ~$100–$150 pp.

Food & Drink

  • 🏖 Resort RestaurantsAll-inclusive resorts typically have 4–8 restaurants included — a main buffet plus à la carte options (steakhouse, Italian, Asian fusion, beachside grill). Barceló and Hard Rock have particularly strong dining programs.
  • 🐟 Captain Cook (Bávaro)One of the best standalone seafood restaurants near the resort strip. Lobster, shrimp, and fresh-caught fish. Good for a night off the all-inclusive.
  • 🥂 La YolaElegant overwater restaurant at Puntacana Resort & Club. Worth a splurge dinner for the setting alone — the pier extends over the marina.
  • 🍹 Beach Bar CrawlMost resorts have 3–5 bars including swim-up bars, beach bars, and lobby bars. All included. Find your favorite and camp out.

Nightlife

  • 🎉 Coco Bongo Punta CanaThe Cancun legend's Dominican outpost — same acrobatics-and-chaos formula, slightly smaller scale. Cover ~$50–$70 with open bar. Worth a night.
  • 🏖 Barceló Pool PartiesBarceló Bávaro is legendary for its pool parties and nighttime entertainment. Even guests at other resorts sometimes pay a day pass to access.
  • 🦇 Imagine DiscoA nightclub literally inside a cave near Punta Cana — stalactites overhead, multiple chambers, and a full DJ setup. One of the more unique club experiences in the Caribbean.
  • 🌴 Jellyfish Beach BarPopular beach club on Bávaro with an open-air vibe, decent food, and nightly live music that attracts a mix of resort guests and long-term travelers.
  • 🎶 Resort ShowsAll-inclusive resorts run nightly entertainment — from live bands to themed parties to acrobatic shows. Not all are great, but it's included and sometimes surprisingly fun.

Practical Tips

PassportApply/renew NOW if needed — processing takes 6–8 weeks minimum.
E-TicketFill out Dominican tourist e-ticket online before travel (free). Required by law.
TippingEven on all-inclusive, tip housekeeping ($2–$5/day) and bartenders ($1/drink) — it matters to staff.
ExcursionsBook independently or through your hotel. Third-party operators are often cheaper than resort desks.
Outside ResortDowntown Punta Cana and Cortecito village are walking distance from Bávaro for a change of scenery.
