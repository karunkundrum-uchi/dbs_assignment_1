Miami

Florida · USA Domestic

Overview

Miami's greatest advantage for a senior trip is also its most underrated: no passport, no customs, no currency exchange, no worrying about international phone plans. You land at MIA or FLL feeling like you've arrived somewhere exotic — because Miami genuinely is exotic, just without the bureaucracy. It is the most cosmopolitan city in the US, a place where Latin America, the Caribbean, and the art world have collided and created something entirely its own.

South Beach is the center of gravity: the pastel Art Deco hotels on Ocean Drive, the widest stretch of white-sand beach in Florida, and a nightlife scene that draws global DJs to venues that don't get started until midnight. But Miami in 2026 is much more than South Beach. Wynwood has transformed from a warehouse district into one of the world's great street art destinations, with Wynwood Walls as the centerpiece and an ever-expanding collection of galleries, bars, and restaurants surrounding it.

Little Havana is the soul of Miami — a neighborhood that moves to its own rhythm, where domino games spill out of Maximo Gomez Park, the coffee is cafecito and colada, and Calle Ocho fills with Latin music on weekend nights. The Everglades are just an hour away and offer one of the most otherworldly landscapes in North America — an airboat ride through the sawgrass at sunset is not something you'll forget. Even Key Biscayne, just over the causeway, has a beach that rivals anything in the Caribbean.

Be warned: June in Miami is seriously hot and humid. Heat indices regularly top 100°F, and afternoon thunderstorms roll in almost daily (typically clearing within an hour). This is the trade-off for lower prices and thinner crowds versus peak winter season. Embrace the heat, stay hydrated, and schedule outdoor activities for early morning or evening.

Top Activities

  • 🏖 Ocean Drive WalkThe Art Deco historic district is one of the great urban walks in America. Best at sunset or late at night when the neon signs light up.
  • 🎨 Wynwood WallsThe world's largest open-air street art museum. Constantly updated, always worth a visit. Surrounding neighborhood has excellent bars and restaurants.
  • 🌿 Everglades Airboat1 hour from Miami. Half-day tours are perfect — airboat through the sawgrass, spot alligators, get a taste of the unique ecosystem. Airboat Rides & Tours operators off US-41.
  • 🥘 Little Havana TourWalk Calle Ocho, stop at Versailles for Cuban food, watch the domino players at Maximo Gomez Park, and try the best colada you'll ever have.
  • ⛵ Biscayne Bay CruiseVarious boat tours take you past the Star Island mansions. Great for a group afternoon on the water.
  • 🏛 Vizcaya MuseumA stunning Italian Renaissance villa and gardens right on the bay. Worth a few hours for architecture lovers.

Food & Drink

  • 🍽 VersaillesThe unofficial Cuban embassy of Miami. Enormous portions of ropa vieja, lechon, and black beans. A true institution. Little Havana.
  • 🦀 Joe's Stone CrabMiami's most legendary restaurant. Expensive and worth it. Make a reservation or hit the takeout window on the side of the building.
  • ☕ Zak the BakerBest breakfast in Wynwood. Fresh-baked bread, great coffee, outstanding sandwiches. Arrives early — it fills up.
  • 🎶 Ball & ChainLittle Havana landmark with live Latin music, good food, and excellent cocktails. The vibe is unmatched.
  • 🐟 La MarPeruvian-inspired seafood by celebrity chef Gastón Acurio at the Mandarin Oriental. Splurge dinner with stunning bay views.

Nightlife

  • 🎉 LIV at FontainebleauOne of the top nightclubs in the US. A-list DJs, two-story space, and consistently ranked among the best clubs in the world. Dress to impress; tickets/guestlist recommended.
  • 🎵 StoryMassive EDM-focused club on Collins Ave. Known for marathon sets from top DJs. Often open until 10am.
  • 🌊 1 Hotel RooftopThe rooftop pool bar at 1 Hotel South Beach is the definitive Miami daytime-into-night experience. Beautiful crowd, beautiful views.
  • 🌴 Mango's Tropical CaféThe quintessential Ocean Drive experience — loud, colorful, Latin music, dancing on the bar. Not for everyone, perfect for the right night.
  • 🎧 Space MiamiLegendary underground club in downtown that goes from Saturday night through Sunday afternoon. Serious music programming.

Practical Tips

Getting AroundSouth Beach is walkable. Use Uber/Lyft for Wynwood, Little Havana, Brickell.
HeatSeriously hot in June. Early morning or evening for outdoor activities.
Beach ChairsBring your own or rent — hotel beach chairs can be $30+/day on South Beach.
ClubsDon't show up before midnight. Guestlist or bottle service gets you in faster.
